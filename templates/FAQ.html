FAQ
Using your algorithm, will everyone end up with the same team?
No, they will not.
The reason being, that we are solving an optimization problem with the constraint that you are having different teams and budgets.
Only teams with the same team and budget will get the same team advised BUT, as you can set your constraints, you are not likely to get a similar team as your friends.

Does statitistics really matter in fantasy football?
We both managed to win our league, using this algorithm, so the short answer is yes.
Football is a game with a lot of randomnesses, and the amount of randomness makes it unlikely that you will win the first round you are using this algorithm.
This algorithm gives you a starting point for making more accurate decisions, that are less biased and with fewer errors than normal. Further, as the season pass by, you will see that as the sample size increases (games), the expected points will go towards the sampled one (highest possible).
Anyone can get lucky in any given week, but statistics tend to win over time (cf. stock market, bookmakers, etc).
How many rounds are you modelling over?
We are modelling for the next 6 rounds, but we are weigthing the first coming rounds higher than the last ones. The reason being that future rounds has more uncertainty to it (injuries, etc.)
